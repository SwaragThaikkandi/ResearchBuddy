Active learning: instead of re-asking about papers it already understands, the model picks the unrated papers in your graph whose verdict would reduce its uncertainty the most (spread of a bootstrap ensemble fitted to your ratings, weighted by relevance). Rating a handful here sharpens every future recommendation. The ± on a match score is that same uncertainty.

Self-tuning — the tool researches itself

      
Inspired by karpathy/autoresearch: perturb one scoring
        parameter, re-measure how well the recommender ranks the papers
        you already rated (AUC/NDCG — offline, deterministic), keep
        the change only if the score genuinely improves, log every
        experiment. Kept changes persist and load on every startup.
        Run overnight without the UI:
        researchbuddy-sentinel --install-task --autotune 20

      

        
        
        
      

      

      

    

  


  
  

    

      
 Sentinel — automatic surveillance

      
When enabled, ResearchBuddy scans your watched topics on
        a schedule by itself (while the UI is running), triages the
        finds with your personal relevance model, files the keepers into the
        inbox below, and writes a dated digest. For scans with the UI closed:
        researchbuddy-sentinel --install-task. Your machine
        watching public literature — nobody watching you.

 Living Graph — a scout that
        explores for you

      
A second, self-optimising graph lives alongside yours.
        Each cycle it acquires new literature (abstracts only),
        prunes itself with graph-theoretic queries (PageRank mass from
        your interests, coverage gaps, diversity), and presents its best
        finds below. Your graph is the prior; the scout is the
        likelihood; your ratings are the evidence — rate a scout paper
        and it flows into your main graph as a Bayesian update
        (abstract-only evidence is deliberately discounted vs full text;
        attach or harvest the PDF later and the discount disappears).

 GROBID
        scientific-PDF parser

      
Dramatically better PDF extraction (sections,
        references, figures, equations). Without it, imports fall back to
        pdfplumber — usable, but shallower graph nodes. Start it BEFORE
        importing PDFs in the Library tab.

How this works (2 minutes)

        
    
          
  1. You and a colleague each run ResearchBuddy. Merging lets
            both of you discover each other's papers — without either
            of you revealing your full reading list (only papers you BOTH
            have are identified; the rest stays private).
    2. 
          
  2. Swap fingerprints once. The code below is your identity
            — like a passport number for your research graph. Read it to
            your colleague over the phone or in person, and note down
            theirs. This is what stops an impostor in the middle.
    3. 
          
  3. Pin them as a peer. "Host" is simply the address of
            their computer — on the same office network it's their machine's
            IP (they can find it with ipconfig); over the
            internet it's whatever address reaches their machine. "Port" is
            just a door number — keep the default 9333 unless it's taken.
    4. 
          
  4. Merge. One of you clicks wait for peer, the other
            picks the pinned peer and clicks Merge. Everything travels
            encrypted between your two machines only — no server, no cloud,
            no files. Both of you get a signed receipt in your ledgers.
    5.
